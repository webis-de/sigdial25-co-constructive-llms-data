What does it mean by human sleep cycle?


0:00:07 EX:
The human sleep cycle, also known as the sleep-wake cycle or circadian rhythm, is the series of physical and mental changes that occur when we sleep. It's a complex process that involves multiple stages, each with distinct brain wave patterns, hormone releases, and physical responses.
A full sleep cycle typically lasts around 90-120 minutes and is divided into three stages of Non-Rapid Eye Movement (NREM) sleep and one stage of Rapid Eye Movement (REM) sleep.
Here's a breakdown of the stages:
**Stage 1 NREM (5-10 minutes):**
- You start to relax and your brain waves slow down.
- Your muscles relax, and you may experience a sense of drifting in and out of consciousness.
**Stage 2 NREM (20-40 minutes):**
- Your body temperature cools down, and your heart rate slows down.
- Your brain waves slow down further, and you become less responsive to your environment.
**Stage 3 NREM (20-30 minutes):**
- This is the deepest stage of NREM sleep, also known as slow-wave sleep.
- Your brain waves slow down even further, and it's difficult to wake up.
- Your body repairs and regenerates tissues, builds bone and muscle, and strengthens your immune system.
**Stage 4 REM (10-30 minutes):**
- You enter a state of rapid eye movement, increased brain activity, and vivid dreams.
- Your brain processes and consolidates memories, and your body paralyzes your muscles to prevent acting out your dreams.
After completing one full cycle, you'll typically enter another cycle, repeating the process throughout the night. Most adults experience 3-5 full sleep cycles per night, with each cycle lasting around 90-120 minutes.
Understanding the human sleep cycle can help you appreciate the importance of getting quality sleep and how it affects your physical and mental well-being.
